## Unicode in Plain English

Unicode is a universal standard that assigns a unique number to every character in every language on Earth — plus thousands of math symbols, emoji, and special letters.

When you use a "fancy font generator," you're not really changing the font. You're swapping regular letters for their Unicode math equivalents. For example:

- Regular A → `U+0041`
- Bold A (𝐀) → `U+1D400`
- Script A (𝒜) → `U+1D49C`

## Why It Pastes Everywhere

Since every device and platform supports Unicode, these characters just work — no fonts to install, no apps to download. Your phone already has the Unicode table built in.

## The Math Alphabet Block

Most fancy text uses the **Mathematical Alphanumeric Symbols** block (U+1D400 to U+1D7FF). It includes:

- Bold, italic, bold-italic
- Script and bold script
- Fraktur and bold fraktur
- Double-struck (𝕝𝕚𝕜𝕖 𝕥𝕙𝕚𝕤)
- Monospace
